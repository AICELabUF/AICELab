  • Learning Analytics Research

    Collaborated with Drexel University and Rutgers University, I am working on a multi-year multi-million NSF grant to design and develop a learning analytics system for a synchronous, technologically mediated math team learning (VMT) environment.

    Read more

  • Scientific Argumentation

    Collaborated with Concord Consortium, University of California Santa Cruz and the National Geographic Society, I am working on a multi-year multi-mission NSF grant to design and develop text mining approaches for students’ written scientific argumentation to automatically discover useful information for teachers and students.

    Read more

  • Game Design & Analytics

    Funded by the U.S. Department of Education (DOE), we designed a 3D collaborative virtual learning environment for teaching social competence to youth with Autism Spectrum Disorders (iSocial).

    I am currently working on the Mission Hydro Science, funded by DOE, to design, develop and evaluate a game-based 3D virtual learning environment for students to learn hydrologic systems and scientific argumentation (Dissertation project).

    Read more

  • Online Communities

    I am examining how cognitive factors, environmental factors, social factors, behavioral factors related with community structure and performance. To reach a more generalizable model, I am pursuing this research program in multi parallel contexts including MOOCs, Social Media, Online Discussion Forums, and Learning Management Systems.

    Read more

Wanli Xing is a PhD Candidate at the University of Missouri-Columbia, iSchool (School of Information Science & Learning Technologies.) His research bridges theories with computational models to inform a data driven approach for education, sociotechnical systems, and design. His scholarly expertise spans across learning science, statistics, design and computer science.
